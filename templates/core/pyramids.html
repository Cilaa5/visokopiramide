The Pyramids

Five ancient structures hidden in the Visoko valley. Each one unique, each one waiting to be explored.

The Bosnian Pyramid complex near Visoko consists of five pyramid-shaped hills, a massive underground tunnel system called Ravne, and numerous other archaeological features. Discovered in 2005 by Dr. Semir Osmanagić, the site has been under excavation ever since and continues to attract visitors, researchers, and curious travellers from across the world.

Visočica hill — the Pyramid of the Sun — is the tallest and most studied of the five structures. At 220 metres, it surpasses the Great Pyramid of Giza (138 m) in height. Its faces align almost perfectly with the four cardinal directions, and excavations have uncovered massive concrete/sandstone blocks on its surface.

Researchers have found stone terraces, a plateau at the top, and unusual electromagnetic and ultrasound readings emanating from the hill. The climb to the top takes about 30–45 minutes and rewards you with a panoramic view over the Visoko valley.

The Pyramid of the Moon (Plješevica hill) is a three-sided structure 190 metres tall. Its northwest face is the most clearly defined, with stone terraces visible after excavation. It sits directly across from the Pyramid of the Sun, creating a dramatic natural amphitheatre.

The climb here is steeper and less crowded than the Sun pyramid, offering a more adventurous experience and excellent views back towards the Sun Pyramid.

🐉

Pyramid of the Dragon

Located to the east, the Pyramid of the Dragon (Buci hill) has a more elongated ridge-like shape. It connects to the underground Ravne tunnel system and contains some of the most interesting stone formations and inscriptions found at the complex.

The site features medieval tombstones (stećci) and is surrounded by forests, making it a beautiful hike even for the casual visitor.

💛

Pyramid of Love

The smallest of the pyramids, the Pyramid of Love has a very regular, heart-shaped formation when viewed from above. It's a shorter hike and ideal for families or visitors with limited mobility.

🌍

Temple of the Earth

More of a flat earthwork than a pyramid, the Temple of the Earth is a large plateau structure. Researchers believe it may have served as a ceremonial or gathering site for the people who built the complex.

Ravne Underground Tunnel Labyrinth

Beneath the pyramid complex lies an extensive network of underground tunnels known as Ravne. The tunnels stretch for over 10 kilometres and are still being excavated. Inside, you'll find large stone monoliths with carved symbols, healing ionised air, high humidity, and a constant cool temperature year-round.

The tunnels are accessible via a guided entry near the town. Around 2 km of tunnels are open to the public. The air quality inside is reportedly exceptional — many visitors describe a strong sense of well-being during their stay.
