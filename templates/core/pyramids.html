Archaeological Complex

The Pyramids

Five ancient structures hidden in the Visoko valley. Each one unique, each one waiting to be explored.

The Bosnian Pyramid complex near Visoko consists of five pyramid-shaped hills, a massive underground tunnel system called Ravne, and numerous other archaeological features. Discovered in 2005 by Dr. Semir Osmanagić, the site continues to attract visitors, researchers, and curious travellers from across the world.

Visočica hill — the Pyramid of the Sun — is the tallest and most studied of the five structures. At 220 metres, it surpasses the Great Pyramid of Giza (138 m) in height. Its faces align almost perfectly with the four cardinal directions, and excavations have uncovered massive sandstone blocks on its surface.

The climb to the top takes about 30–45 minutes and rewards you with a panoramic view over the Visoko valley. Researchers have found unusual electromagnetic and ultrasound readings emanating from the structure.

The Pyramid of the Moon (Plješevica hill) is a three-sided structure 190 metres tall. Its northwest face is the most clearly defined, with stone terraces visible after excavation.

The climb here is steeper and less crowded than the Sun Pyramid, offering a more adventurous experience and excellent views back across the valley.

🐉

Pyramid of the Dragon

Located to the east, the Pyramid of the Dragon (Buci hill) has a more elongated ridge-like shape. It connects to the underground Ravne tunnel system and contains some of the most interesting stone formations and inscriptions found at the complex.

The site features medieval tombstones (stećci) and is surrounded by forests, making it a beautiful hike even for casual visitors.

💛

Pyramid of Love

The smallest of the five, with a very regular heart-shaped formation when viewed from above. A shorter, family-friendly hike ideal for all ages.

🌍

Temple of the Earth

A large plateau earthwork rather than a classic pyramid. Researchers believe it served as a ceremonial gathering site for the people who built the complex.

Underground

Ravne Tunnel
Labyrinth

Beneath the pyramid complex lies an extensive network of tunnels known as Ravne. The tunnels stretch for over 10 kilometres and are still being excavated. Inside, you'll find large stone monoliths with carved symbols, healing ionised air, and a constant cool temperature.

Around 2 km are open to the public via guided tours. The air quality inside is exceptional — many visitors describe a remarkable sense of well-being.
